Consensus Algorithms in Blockchain

Every blockchain has to solve the same problem: get thousands of strangers to agree on one shared history, with no referee in charge. Select a mechanism below to see exactly how it works, what it trades away, and which real networks actually run on it.

Distributed-systems researchers describe blockchain design as a trilemma: a network can push hard on Scalability (how much it can process), Security (how expensive it is to attack) and Decentralisation (how many independent parties keep it honest) - but optimising two usually pulls against the third. Every consensus mechanism below is really just a different, deliberate answer to that trade-off.

Six mechanisms are compared here - the ones that actually secure the blockchains and cryptocurrencies in circulation today. Pick one to update the mechanism, trilemma scores, real-world chains, and language notes below. The compatibility matrix near the bottom stays visible for all six at once.
